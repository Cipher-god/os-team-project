OPTIMAL PAGE REPLACEMENT ALGORITHM
OPR
Basic Theory :
  Peterson's formula was designed to work with just two processes but has since been generalized to accommodate multiple processes.
It would be possible to avoid race conditions if no two processes were ever at a critical stage simultaneously. To solve the critical section problem of mutual exclusion, we need four conditions to be true.
  1. During critical sections, two processes cannot run simultaneously.
  2. It is not assumed that processes are running at the same speed or on multiple CPUs.
  3. There should be no process blocking another process.
  4. It is important not to act arbitrarily long before entering a critical section in a process.
  • Initialize the shared variables turn and flag to a known state (e.g., turn = 1, flag[0] = flag[1] = false).
  • Each process sets its flag to true to indicate it wants to enter the critical section.
  • Each process sets turn to the other process's id to give the other process priority if it also wants to enter the critical section.
  • Each process enters a busy-waiting loop while the other process's flag is true and it is not its turn.
  • Once a process has exited the busy-waiting loop, it enters the critical section to execute the protected code.
  • When a process is done executing the critical section, it sets its flag to false to indicate it no longer needs access and gives the turn to the other process.
  • The other process can now enter its critical section, or continue to busy-wait if it still needs to wait.
